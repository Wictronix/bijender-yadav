# Bijender Yadav Website — Content Strategy & Copy Framework

---

## 1. Content Philosophy

This is not a party brochure. It is a **personal political brand** built on three pillars:

1. **Authenticity** — Real stories from Moradabad, real issues, real voice
2. **Credibility** — News coverage, Wikipedia presence, alliance backing
3. **Urgency** — The 2027 election is coming. Change is needed. Join now.

All content must serve one of three goals:
- **Inform** → Who is Bijender Yadav and what does he stand for?
- **Trust** → Why should Moradabad believe him?
- **Activate** → What can a supporter do right now?

---

## 2. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
- **Warm, direct, grounded** — speaks like a community leader, not a press release
- **Hindi-first** — even English content should feel natural, not translated-bureaucratic
- **People-centric** — always about Moradabad's people, not about Bijender's ambitions

### Tone by Section
| Section | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Hero | Inspirational, confident, aspirational |
| About / Bio | Personal, humble, authentic |
| Issues | Serious, factual, solution-focused |
| News | Neutral, journalistic |
| Join / CTA | Urgent, warm, inclusive |
| Alliance | Unified, political, forward-looking |

### Banned phrases (avoid)
- "I will transform Moradabad" (too vague, overused)
- "Vote for change" alone (needs specificity)
- "Humble servant of the people" (cliché)
- Any mention of opponents by name on the website (stays positive)

## 7. Wikipedia Page Guidelines

**Article title:** Bijender Yadav (politician)

**Minimum sections to create:**
- Early life
- Political career
- Party affiliation (Indian National Congress)
- Constituency (Moradabad)
- Positions and views
- References (press coverage, INC press releases)

**Key rule:** Wikipedia requires neutral point of view (NPOV) and verifiable sources. All claims must link to published news articles. No promotional language.

**Strategy:** Start adding references to Bijender's name in press releases so that Wikipedia citations can be sourced back to published media.
